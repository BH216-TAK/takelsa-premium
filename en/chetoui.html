The Chaïbi — a northern variety, most often milled warm elsewhere. Here, for the first time, cold-extracted: revealing a pronounced green fruitiness — cut grass, crushed leaf, raw artichoke.

On the palate, the pungency is clean and the bitterness holds — the mark of polyphenols. Tunisia's second oil-olive variety, naturally rich in polyphenols. Milled within six to eight hours of picking, to keep the freshness intact.
